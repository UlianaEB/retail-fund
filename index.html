The Strategy

Three ways this deal makes money

Income from day one, growing returns through rent increases, and a value uplift realised at sale or through an equity release once the asset is refinanced at its stabilised value.

01

Immediate diversified income

Six established tenants deliver $529,000 net rental income from settlement. Four leases carry CPI linked annual increases, so income grows each year without active intervention.

02

Two anchor tenants

One anchor signed an eight year lease in 2023 with yearly CPI increases and a renewal right in 2031. The other anchor's lease expired in 2019 and it has occupied month to month since. A new lease of at least three years at market rent, with management expenses included, is a condition of settlement.

03

Sell a stabilised asset

The purchase price reflects a 9.5% yield while the body corporate and one anchor lease remain unresolved. The exit is modelled at a 7.0% yield, the level a professionally managed and fully leased asset of this type can command.

Why the price sits below asking

The body corporate is self managed by the current owner and one anchor tenant has occupied month to month since its lease expired in 2019. Both factors narrow the pool of buyers at the asking yield. The fund will appoint a professional body corporate manager and secure the anchor lease before settlement. Once resolved, the same asset supports a materially stronger value.

Body Corporate

Body corporate

The body corporate is currently self managed by the vendor. The fund will appoint a professional body corporate manager and put a funded maintenance plan in place, with the key positions confirmed during due diligence.

Why this matters to the price

Buyers pay tighter yields for properties with professional body corporate management and a clear maintenance record. Self management narrows the buyer pool and holds the price down. Resolving it is a core part of the value plan.

What we resolve during DD

Appointment of a professional manager, a maintenance plan with funding, and confirmation of the body corporate's creditor and levy positions. These are secured as conditions before the purchase goes unconditional.

Risks

An honest assessment

Tenant vacancy or default
If tenants vacate or fail to pay rent, distributable income falls and distributions reduce or pause. This is the main operating risk of the investment.
Anchor leases at exit
Both anchor leases face events near the target Year 5 exit. The lease secured at settlement runs at least three years and may be at or past expiry by sale. The second anchor's eight year term reaches its renewal right in mid 2031. The modelled exit yield depends on both anchors having committed lease terms in place at sale.
Exit yield
The 7.0% exit yield is the most significant assumption in the model. A sale at 7.5% would reduce the exit value to around $8.41M and the partners' surplus share by around $464,000.
Body corporate
Actions such as appointing a manager and setting budgets require body corporate resolutions. The fund's ability to deliver these depends on the ownership and voting interest acquired, confirmed during due diligence and secured as conditions of purchase.
Maintenance
Some maintenance has been deferred under self management. A building condition report during due diligence will confirm the scope and cost before the purchase goes unconditional.
Finance
Debt terms are indicative. Final structure, rate and covenants depend on bank approval. Materially different terms would reduce distributable income. The offer will be conditional on finance.
Interest rates
The model assumes 6.5% through the hold. Higher rates reduce distributable income and partner distributions.
Liquidity
Partnership interests are not freely tradeable. Partners should treat this as illiquid capital for the full hold. The partnership agreement will include a mechanism for one partner to exit at an independently assessed value, though this is not a guarantee of liquidity.
