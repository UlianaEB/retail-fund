The Strategy
Three ways this deal makes money
Income from day one, growing returns through rent increases, and a value uplift at exit driven by a stronger rent roll.
01
Immediate diversified income
Seven established tenants across retail, healthcare and commercial deliver $528,806 net rental income from settlement. Four leases carry inflation-linked annual increases, so the income grows automatically each year without any active intervention.
02
Anchor tenant lease formalisation
The anchor retail tenant currently occupies on a month-to-month basis at $118,000 per year. Based on comparable Johnsonville leases, market rent for this space sits at $284 per square metre — formalising at market adds ~$22,000 to annual net income immediately. Three further tenants also sit below comparable market rates, with conservative reversion adding a further ~$12,500 at lease reviews from Year 3.
03
Sell at a stronger yield
Purchased at 8.0%, the property is expected to be sold at a 7.0% yield — reflecting a stronger rent roll, formalised leases, and natural yield compression over the hold period. This spread alone is worth ~$2.17M in gross value creation. The sale is investor-voted and can happen at any time, including before Year 5 if the right offer comes in.

Why the exit is where the real money is made
Acquired at an 8.0% yield. By Year 5, the rent roll has grown to $631,000 through anchor tenant formalisation, inflation-linked increases and market rent reversion on three tenants. Selling at a 7.0% yield values the property at $9.01M — a gross uplift of $2.40M on purchase price. After debt repayment and disposal costs, investors share net sale proceeds of $5.21M across 35 units. The exit is investor-voted and can be triggered at any time — the 5-year horizon is a guide, not a lock-in.
