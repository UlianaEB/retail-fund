The Opportunity
Three reasons this deal works
A well-established multi-tenanted retail and commercial complex in Wellington, New Zealand. Strong arterial road frontage, on-site car parking, and a seismic rating above the Wellington threshold. Income from day one, growing returns through rent increases, and a compelling exit driven by a stronger rent roll.
01
Immediate, diversified income
Seven established tenants including two national anchor retailers across retail, healthcare and commercial deliver $560,000 net income from settlement. Four leases grow automatically with inflation every year with no negotiation required.
02
Rents growing throughout the hold
The anchor retail tenant is currently on a month-to-month arrangement at below-market rent. Formalisation at market rates is structured as a condition of settlement, locking in an immediate income uplift of ~$22,000 per year before the purchase completes. Three further tenants reset to market rates at lease renewal from Year 3.
03
Exit at a tighter yield
Purchased at 8.0%, the property is expected to sell at a 7.0% yield at Year 5, reflecting a stronger, fully stabilised rent roll and natural yield compression over the hold period. The exit is jointly agreed and can occur at any time if a compelling offer is received.

How It Works
How your return is calculated
Every dollar follows a simple sequence before any surplus is shared. Capital partners are paid first at every step.
Income waterfall — applied every quarter
1
Finance costs paid
Interest on the investment property finance is paid first from rental income. $257,790 per year, leaving $302,210 available for distribution in Year 1.
2
Operating costs paid
Property operating costs including property management are paid from rental income.
3
Your 7% preferred return
Capital partners receive their 7% preferred return first before the working partner receives anything above their management fee.
4
Surplus split 70/30
Any income remaining after steps 1 to 3 is split 70% to capital partners, 30% to the working partner.
The working partner's management fee of 1% per year is paid from the surplus at step 4, after capital partners have received their preferred return.

Risks
An honest assessment
Every investment carries risk. These are the material risks in this deal and how they are managed.
Exit yield
The 7.0% exit yield is a projection, not a guarantee. If Wellington commercial market conditions soften and the property sells at 7.5% instead, the exit value reduces to approximately $8.41M, reducing capital partners' gain share by around $195,000.
Interest rates
The finance rate of 6.5% per year is fixed at the model rate. If rates rise during the hold period, net distributable income would reduce. A 0.5% rate increase would reduce annual distributions to capital partners by approximately $19,830.
Vacancy
Seven tenants across multiple categories means no single tenant departure materially threatens the overall income. The largest tenant represents 30% of total income. All tenants are established operators with existing leases in place at settlement.
Early exit
Neither party can force a sale. If a capital partner requires liquidity before Year 5, the Joint Venture Agreement includes a buyout mechanism allowing one party to purchase the other's interest at an independently assessed market value.
Conservative modelling: All rent reversion figures are based on the lowest comparable market rates, not the highest. Even without any reversion whatsoever, the 7% preferred return to capital partners is fully covered from passing rents alone from day one.
